# Industry Gross Margin Benchmarks

Use these as **rough reference ranges only** when explaining whether an SMB owner's margins look typical, high, or concerning. They are **not targets the owner must hit, and not an authoritative benchmark** — they are broad, general industry ranges (not China-specific), and real margins vary widely by industry sub-category, region, business model, and data source. Sources differ greatly. Never present any specific figure here as an authoritative standard; always verify against the owner's own industry and local (regional) context before drawing a conclusion.

Always frame benchmarks as context that still needs local verification, not a verdict: "For your kind of business, gross margins often land somewhere around 40–55% — but that's a rough, general range, not a China-specific standard, so treat it as a checkpoint to verify, not a judgment. You're at 38%; let's look at what that means for your pricing scenarios."

## Benchmarks by category

| Category | Rough Range (verify locally) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Retail — General** | 40–55% | Varies widely by product category |
| **Retail — Apparel / Fashion** | 45–65% | Higher for brands, lower for resellers |
| **Retail — Food & Grocery** | 20–35% | Thin margins; volume-dependent |
| **E-commerce** | 30–50% | Depends on fulfillment costs |
| **Restaurant / Food Service** | 60–70% | Gross margin on food; net is much lower after labor |
| **Professional Services** | 60–80% | Consulting, legal, accounting, design |
| **Skilled Trades** | 35–55% | Plumbing, electrical, HVAC; materials-heavy |
| **Cleaning / Maintenance Services** | 40–60% | Labor-intensive |
| **Health & Wellness** | 50–70% | Fitness studios, massage, coaching |
| **SaaS / Software** | 70–85% | Software delivery cost is low; high gross margins are expected |
| **Wholesale / Distribution** | 20–35% | Low margins; depends on volume and terms |
| **Manufacturing** | 25–45% | Varies by product complexity |
| **Construction** | 20–35% | Project-based; materials-heavy |

## How to use these

1. **Match the owner's industry** using what they've already told you in conversation, or ask directly if it's unclear.
2. **State the range, not a single number.** Margins vary within categories, and by region and business model — present a band and say it's approximate.
3. **Flag when margins are well below the floor.** Under 20% gross margin in most service categories usually means the owner is underpricing or missing cost items.
4. **Don't cite a specific source, and don't treat these as authoritative or China-specific.** They are general industry knowledge, not live data feeds — verify against the owner's actual industry and region before drawing conclusions.

## When benchmarks don't fit

Some SMBs run hybrid models (e.g., a yoga studio that also sells retail apparel). In those cases, segment the analysis if possible, or note: "Your business spans multiple categories — I'll use the benchmarks for each revenue stream separately."
